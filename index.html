Basic multiplying

To multiply faster we must
do it the same way we read
from left to right. We also can estimate
much faster.

Example: 126 X 3
1: 3 x 100 = 300 (we are actually straing with
100 becasuse the 1 is in the hundreds place)
2: 3 x 20 = 60
3: 3 x 6 = 18
4: 300 + 60 + 18 = [378: Our Awnser]
Tip: Don't forget to carry the one from
the 18 over to the 6 from 60

Squaring two digit numbers

Squaring numbers quickly is only a matter
of practice. The first thing is find the closest
number in base 10(eg: 10, 20, 30).

Example: 43
1: Nearest base for 43 is 40
2: If you go down 3 from 43 to 40 you must
also go up 3 to 46
3: Then you multiply 40 x 46 (Tip: all you
really have to do is multiply 4 x 46
and add a zreo add the end)
4: Add your answer from step 3 and
the square root of how much you went down or up in
this case from step 1 we went down 3
{1840 + 9 = [1849: Our Answer]}

Simple Tricks

Two simple tricks for multiplying and
squaring two digit number. First is multiplying a two digit number
by 11. The second is squaring a two digit
number ending in 5.

Example for multiplying by 11:24
1: First split the number like this 2_4
2: Second multiply the number 2 x 4 = 8
3: Third put the answer for 2 in the middle
of your original number [284: Our Answer]
